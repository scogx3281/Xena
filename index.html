Who are we?

Cameron Heights Collegiate Institute (CHCI) is a high school in downtown Kitchener, Ontario, Canada. Our mission as CHCI Programming Club is to promote an interest in computer science and software development in our local community.

To do so, we run different activities:

  • Contest preparation
  • CamHacks
  • Community projects

Contest preparation

The CCC is Canada's largest annual secondary school computing contest. To help CHCI students prepare for the contest, we post weekly problems to Google Classroom (class code: t6yzm5x) and award points based on their completion. Each semester, the highest scoring members are given prizes as a reward for their effort.

CamHacks

Every year, we run a twelve-hour hackathon at the school as an event to help involve members of the community in software development. Groups are permitted to have up to four members and have nine hours to build a project that is judged at the end of the day. The winning team receives a prize, and everyone receives free food.

Community projects

We believe the structured nature of contest problems and hackathons isn't enough to interest people in software development. This is where community projects come in. Any local non-profit organization or school club can contact us for help with a software project, such as a website. Applying our members' skills lets them gain valuable experience while simultaneously benefitting the community.
